Cyber Strategy

A cybersecurity strategy is a high-level plan for securing and protecting an organization's assets and operations. Because technology and cyber threats are constantly expanding and changing, cyber strategy will almost probably need to be updated and evolved. A cybersecurity strategy isn't supposed to be perfect; it's merely a well-informed judgement as to what should be done. The goal of establishing and implementing a cybersecurity plan is to better secure assets, crown jewels, and anything else that is critical to the operation of a corporation. A robust cyber strategy can help organisations protect their reputation and reduce harm to themselves, their workers, customers, partners, and others by preventing minor issues from becoming large ones.

Risk and Governance

Risk and governance refers to identifying the potential risks that are associated with an organisations rules conventions, processes, mechanisms and the implementations to combat them. The goal of risk and governance is to prevent and/or reduce the potential human and economic costs that would come with a disaster. Not only does risk governance analysis the traditional risks of an institution but considers various stakeholders, broader legal, political, economic and social contexts in which a risk can be evaluated and managed.

Zero Trust Security

In the current circumstances of the world, todays organisations need a security model that adapts to the modern environment, helping to protect people, devices, applications and so much more, no matter where they are located thus embracing the mobile workforce. This is the innards of Zero trust.

The Zero Trust model assumes a breach and evaluates each request as if it came from an uncontrolled network, rather than thinking everything behind the corporate firewall is safe. Zero Trust teaches us to "never trust, always verify," regardless of where the request originates or what resource it accesses.

Internet of Things

The Internet of Things is all about linking things utilising low-power wide-area network (LPWAN) connectivity to build a smart connected environment employing new technology over a wide area network.

Low-power wide area network (LPWAN) is a wireless wide area network technology that connects low-bandwidth, battery-powered devices over large distances. LPWANs are less expensive and more energy efficient than typical mobile networks.

Multicloud Strategy

Multi-cloud / hybrid architecture allows businesses to distribute workloads over several cloud environments as well as on-premises infrastructure, allowing them to get the most bang for their buck while reducing risks associated with particular cloud environments.

All cloud services are vulnerable to outages. Although technically valid, industry laggards frequently exploit this argument to discourage cloud adoption. Even if there are real-world examples of cloud outages to back up this claim, the cloud industry already has potential solutions in place to address these issues.

Progressive enterprises who wish to move to the cloud without putting all their eggs in one basket can use this tool so they can use a multi-cloud environment to do precisely that. This value proposition alone warrants multi-cloud infrastructure solutions' extensive growth and adoption in the future.
